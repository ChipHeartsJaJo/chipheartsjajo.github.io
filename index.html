Our Love Story...

From Coffee Meets Bagel to I Do! It is amazing how much we have done and seen together since our first date at Blue Jacket back in May of 2014. We are so excited about having all of our family and friends join us as we make a commitment to each other and start the next chapter of our life together as husband and wife. We hope our website will tell you a little more about us as well as provide you with all of the information you need for the weekend.

I do!

Laura Morgan

It wasn’t long after I met Laura that I knew my life would be different and that knowing her and loving her would change me forever. And I wasn’t wrong. She’s a wonderful, generous, and talented person who fills my world with great beauty.

Her integrity, generosity, tenderness and affection embeds in me a sense of great purpose and calmness. And there’s no one I’d rather have in my corner or in my life.

I’m a lucky guy to get to wake up each day next to this beautiful person.

Me too!

Christian Kessler

If I was asked to describe Christian in three words they would be kind, thoughtful, and intelligent. Early in our relationship I knew that he was special, and as we got to know each other better, it became apparent how much we had in common, from our love of politics to wimpy sushi.

Even though we may not share the same love for flying or views on technology, I feel like we complement each other well and our relationship is a true partnership. He makes me a better person and challenges me to take new adventures and try new things.

I feel so lucky that I have Christian in my life. I cannot wait to marry my best friend.

And in this crazy life, and through these crazy times, it's you, it's you, you make me sing, you're every line, you're every word, you're everything.
2014

12 May: First Date

We first met virtually, on a dating app called Coffee Meets Bagel.  After a few days of texting to get to know each other, we met in person for drinks at Blue Jacket down in Yards Park.

We quickly discovered our Oklahoma connection: Christian grew up in a town in Oklahoma very close to where Laura’s grandfather lived.  We also discovered our mutual love for politics, college football, and the West Wing.

26 June: First Trip

For our first trip together, we went to Key West for a long weekend.  Christian took his first “road trip” driving from Miami down to Key West.  We both had growing experiences on this trip.  Christian fed a tarpon and spent some quality time at a turtle rescue.

Laura let someone else do the driving and rode in a very small airplane.  We enjoyed the southernmost point, good food, and great conversation.  This trip would be the first of many traveling adventures together.

14 August: First I Love You

Christian convinced Laura, who doesn’t like to fly, to take a 10-hour plane ride to the beautiful state of Hawaii for a cruise of the four major islands.  Our last stop was on the beautiful island of Kauai.  We were enjoying a wonderful sunset dinner over looking the ocean when Christian said I love you.  It was the perfect ending to a wonderful vacation.  

25 October: Pumpkin Patch: Party of Three

The first outing the three of us took was to the pumpkin patch.  We took turns pulling Riley in a wagon through the pumpkin patch in search of the perfect pumpkin.  We also took the first photo of the three of us that day.  At Riley’s request, the purchase of chocolate cupcakes at checkout was a must. 

2015

17 February: That Time We Bought a House

It was Friday night and we were enjoying a Valentine’s Day dinner one night early.  We began talking about possible ideas for a living situation when Christian’s lease was up. The idea was brought up about Laura selling her townhouse and buying a place together.

We spent the rest of the night looking areas in Northern Virginia with good school pyramids and potential listing.  Less than 72 hours later we had signed a contract on a new home in Springfield.

25 April: She Said Yes!

It was the weekend of Christian’s birthday and his parents had come to visit from Georgia.  Laura thought they were coming to help get her townhouse ready to put on the market and celebrate Christian’s birthday.  They were actually in town because Christian had planned on proposing to Laura on his birthday.  After going to a WWII film at the Smithsonian Museum, we headed to Christian’s apartment to get some boxes.

Christian’s apartment was decorating with flowers and candles.  There was a book on a table in the middle of his apartment.  It was a photo book Christian had made called “Three of Us.”  The book told the story of us and contained pictures of our time together.  When Laura turned to a page in the book it had a picture of a man on one knee in front of a woman proposing.  Laura looked over and Christian was on one knee and asked her to marry him.  She was completely surprised and said yes!  They retuned to Laura’s parents house to celebrate.
